Kirigami Solar Cells

Overview

The problem at hand that we were looking to solve was that current solar panels were too inefficient. With tracking and magnification technologies our team could greatly increase the percent of solar energy collected. I joined the team at a point where the technology could be proven to work by Professor Shtein's team of grad students, yet we still needed to develop the idea into a marketable product.

My Contributions

Somehow the user had to control the solar panels and get data about how much solar power the solar panel collected. Thus, I created an Android App that communicates with an Arduino (which in turn communicates with the solar panel). This app is able to send over commands via Bluetooth and receive data in the form of byte arrays which are then parsed and displayed to the the user. Initially the app was implemented using Firebase, yet to avoid the case where the user is unable to access wifi, we switched to Bluetooth. By just setting your phone down on the roof upon installation, the app could calculate the angle and send this information over to the Arduino for callibration purposes.

Other Stuff

Kirigami Solar is a team under the Multidisciplinary Design Program at the University of Michigan led by Professor Max Shtein and is eventually looking to become a startup.
